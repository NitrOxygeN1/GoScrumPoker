Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how GoScrumPoker (“we”, “us”, or “the Service”) collects, uses, and protects information when you use our planning-poker web application, including when you sign in with Google.

1. Who we are

GoScrumPoker is a real-time Scrum Poker tool for agile teams. The Service is provided as hosted software; the operator is the entity that deploys and maintains your instance (for example, the project maintainer listed on the deployment or OAuth consent screen).

2. Information we collect

2.1 Google Sign-In (OAuth)

If you choose to sign in with Google, we use Google OAuth 2.0 and receive profile information from Google that you authorize, typically including:

  • Google account ID (subject identifier)
  • Email address
  • Display name
  • Profile picture URL

We do not receive your Google password. Google's use of your data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

2.2 Room and session data

When you use poker rooms without signing in, we may process:

  • A display name you enter for the room
  • A random client identifier stored in your browser (sessionStorage)
  • Vote values and room state needed to run the session

2.3 Technical data

Our servers may log standard request metadata (such as IP address, user agent, timestamps, and error details) for security, reliability, and debugging. Logs are retained only as long as needed for those purposes.

3. How we use information

  • Authenticate you and maintain your signed-in session
  • Display your name and avatar to you (and where applicable, in the product UI)
  • Operate real-time rooms, votes, and WebSocket synchronization
  • Protect the Service against abuse and diagnose failures
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not sell your personal information.

4. Legal bases (EEA/UK users)

Where applicable, we process personal data on the basis of contract performance (providing the Service), legitimate interests (security and improvement), and consent (where you initiate Google Sign-In).

5. Storage and retention

Signed-in profile data may be stored in server memory or a database configured by the operator. Room data may be stored in memory, Redis, or PostgreSQL depending on deployment. Retention periods depend on configuration; ephemeral deployments may discard data on restart. Session cookies expire according to server settings (typically up to seven days).

6. Cookies

We use cookies for:

  • Session — keeps you signed in after Google authentication (HttpOnly, Secure in production)
  • OAuth state — short-lived cookie used only during the Google login flow

When the app is embedded (for example, in Google Meet), session cookies may use SameSite=None; Secure so authentication works inside an iframe.

7. Third parties

  • Google — OAuth and user profile APIs when you sign in with Google
  • Hosting providers — infrastructure used to run the Service (e.g. cloud hosting)

We do not share personal data with advertisers. Data is disclosed only when required by law or to protect rights and safety.

8. Security

We use HTTPS in production, signed session cookies, and industry-standard practices for OAuth. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; use the Service at your own risk appropriate to your organization's requirements.

9. Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal data, and to withdraw consent. To exercise these rights, contact us via the Support page. You may also revoke the app's access in your Google Account permissions.

10. Children

The Service is not directed at children under 13 (or 16 in the EEA). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

11. International transfers

Data may be processed in countries where our hosting providers operate. Appropriate safeguards may apply depending on your jurisdiction and provider agreements.

12. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when we do. Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

13. Contact

Questions about this policy: see our Support page.
