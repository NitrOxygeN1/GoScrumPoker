Beta testing opt-out

GoScrumPoker is beta software. You can stop testing at any time using the steps below.

After you complete these steps, we no longer receive new data from your use of the app. Data already stored may remain until it is deleted by the operator or expires; see the Privacy Policy for details.

1. Leave beta testing

If you joined as a tester for the Google Meet add-on or OAuth draft app:

  1. Stop opening the add-on — you do not need to take further action in Meet if you only want to stop using it during calls.
  2. Remove yourself as a test user (if applicable) — the app operator may have added your Google account under Google Cloud Console → APIs & Services → OAuth consent screen → Test users. Ask the operator to remove your email, or decline any invite to test.
  3. Leave a tester group — if access was granted via a Google Group or Workspace test program, leave that group or ask the admin to remove you.
  4. Uninstall the Meet add-on and revoke OAuth access using the sections below so the app cannot access your Google account.

Questions? See Support.

2. Uninstall the Google Meet add-on

How to remove Go Scrum Poker from Google Meet depends on how it was installed.

During a meeting (most testers)

  1. Join or start a Google Meet call.
  2. Open Activities (square grid icon) or Apps in the meeting controls.
  3. Find Go Scrum Poker (or the name shown in the add-on list).
  4. Close the add-on side panel, then open add-on settings if shown and choose Remove, Uninstall, or Don't show again (wording varies by Meet version).

From your Google Account (installed add-ons)

  1. Open Google Account → Third-party apps with account access.
  2. Look for entries related to Go Scrum Poker or GoScrumPoker.
  3. Select the app and choose Remove access (this also revokes OAuth; see below).

Workspace users (company-managed Meet)

If your organization installed the add-on for everyone, you may not be able to uninstall it yourself. Contact your Google Workspace administrator and ask them to remove the Go Scrum Poker Meet add-on from your domain or OU.

3. Revoke OAuth permissions

Revoking access removes the app's permission to read your Google profile (name, email, avatar) used for sign-in.

  1. Go to myaccount.google.com/permissions.
  2. Under Third-party apps with account access, select Go Scrum Poker or the client name shown for this app.
  3. Click Remove access and confirm.

You can also sign out in the app via Logout if that option is available at /logout on the deployment you use. Signing out clears the session cookie but does not by itself revoke Google OAuth — use Google Account permissions for a full revoke.

4. Clear local browser data (optional)

The planning-poker UI may store a display name or room preference in your browser (sessionStorage / localStorage). To clear it:

  • Clear site data for goscrumpoker.onrender.com (or your deployment host) in browser settings, or
  • Use a private/incognito window when trying the app again later.
